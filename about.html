LAstarworks — Goran Mandić (Limeniks)

LAstarworks is a long‑term, fully independent creative and technical practice founded and built by Goran Mandić, who works under the creative name Limeniks. Born from years of experimental work under Limeniks Artworks, LAstarworks is where visual storytelling, hands‑on engineering and curiosity meet — a single‑person laboratory for making, learning and sharing.

Over a decade of photography, video, 3D printing, DIY engineering and STEM exploration shaped one clear idea: to design and build a personal astronomical observatory that is both a working scientific instrument and an immersive art installation. LAstarworks is not a product or a hobby; it is a deliberate practice — a public record of a maker’s process, failures, iterations and discoveries.

What LAstarworks does

  • Designs and builds DIY telescope mounts, domes and imaging rigs.
  • Produces astrophotography and long‑exposure work that documents the sky and the making process.
  • Explores STEM through practical engineering, open‑source tools and educational experiments.
  • Creates site‑specific art by integrating the observatory into a living permaculture garden.
  • Shares process and learning through visual storytelling, tutorials and project documentation.

Why it matters

LAstarworks bridges two worlds that rarely meet: the precision of scientific instrumentation and the ambiguity of artistic practice. The observatory will sit in the middle of a garden, surrounded by vegetables, trees and permaculture systems — a deliberate contrast of soil and sky, technology and ecology. The result is a place that functions as a scientific tool, a creative studio and a public artwork.

Who makes it

Every stage of LAstarworks — from concept and CAD to welding, electronics, imaging and branding — is conceived and executed by one person: Goran Mandić (Limeniks). The project is an argument for what a single dedicated maker can achieve when curiosity, craft and persistence are combined.

Invitation

LAstarworks documents the journey. If you’re interested in DIY astronomy, hands‑on STEM, or the intersection of art and ecology, follow the project, explore the gallery and join the conversation.
